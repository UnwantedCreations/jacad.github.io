OET

  • What is OET?

    OET, which stands for Occupational English Test, is an English language proficiency assessment designed specifically for healthcare professionals. It evaluates their ability to communicate effectively in English within a healthcare setting. OET is widely recognized and accepted by regulatory healthcare bodies in various English-speaking countries.

  • What are the modules in OET?

    OET comprises four modules that assess different language skills crucial for healthcare professionals. These modules include:

    • Listening: This section evaluates the ability to understand spoken English in various healthcare contexts. It consists of three parts and takes approximately 45 minutes.
    • Reading: This module tests the comprehension of written English in healthcare-related texts. It also consists of three parts and has a duration of 60 minutes.
    • Writing: In this module, candidates are required to compose a letter based on a given case scenario relevant to their specific profession. This task assesses writing skills and has a duration of 45 minutes.
    • Speaking: This module involves a role-play scenario where the candidate interacts with an interlocutor, simulating a consultation with a patient, client, or carer. It lasts approximately 20 minutes.
  • What are the scores required for OET?

    The scores needed in OET vary based on the healthcare profession and the country where the candidate intends to work. However, a general benchmark is a score of 350 or higher, which is equivalent to a grade B. The scores are reported on a scale from 0 to 500, with 500 being the highest and 0 the lowest. These numeric scores are mapped to letter grades ranging from A (highest) to E (lowest). It's important to note that OET does not provide an overall grade; instead, it assesses each module individually.

OSCE

  • What is OSCE?

    OSCE, which stands for Objective Structured Clinical Examination, is a comprehensive assessment method used in healthcare education. It evaluates the clinical competence and skills of healthcare professionals through a series of structured stations or scenarios, where candidates interact with standardized patients or perform specific tasks.

  • Why is OSCE Important?

    OSCE is important because it provides a standardized and objective way to assess the clinical skills and competencies of healthcare professionals. It ensures that candidates can apply their knowledge in real-world clinical scenarios and interact effectively with patients.

  • What Does the Program Cover?

    The OSCE preparation program covers various aspects including communication skills, clinical reasoning, physical examination techniques, patient interaction, and scenario-based problem-solving. It also focuses on time management and organization during the examination.
